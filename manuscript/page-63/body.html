Chapter The Ninth.

(After having tramped nearly all over the city, I caught sight of Giton, as though through a fog, standing at the end of the street, (on the very threshold of the inn, and I hastened to the same place. When I inquired whether my “brother” had prepared anything for breakfast, the boy sat down upon the bed and wiped away the trickling tears with his thumb. I was greatly disturbed by such conduct on the part of my “brother,” and demanded to be told what had happened.
